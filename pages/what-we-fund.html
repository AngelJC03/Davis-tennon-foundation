What We Fund

The Davis-Tennon Foundation is committed to driving meaningful change by funding initiatives that address critical needs within Rhode Island communities. Our goal is to empower individuals and families, remove barriers to opportunity, and create lasting solutions for underserved populations.

Our Funding Priorities

Empowering Women

Empowering Women

We support programs that uplift and empower women through education, leadership training, workforce development, and resources to help them achieve independence and stability.

Domestic Violence Awareness

Preventing Domestic Violence

We fund initiatives that provide critical resources for survivors, promote prevention education, support intervention efforts, and create safer communities for all.

Student Opportunities

Student Opportunities

We are passionate about funding programs that enrich students’ lives outside of school hours, such as after-school programs, mentorship opportunities, and access to the arts and STEM education.

Housing Stability

Housing Stability

We invest in efforts that address housing challenges, including affordable housing projects, emergency shelter programs, and initiatives that combat homelessness in our communities.

Funding Opportunities

The Davis-Tennon Foundation accepts Letters of Intent (LOIs) on a quarterly basis to support initiatives aligned with our mission of empowering women, preventing domestic violence, supporting student enrichment outside the classroom, and promoting housing stability.

Please note: Funding is not offered on a rolling basis. All organizations must submit a new LOI for each cycle in which they wish to be considered, regardless of previous submissions or funding history.

Quarterly LOI Deadlines


  • Fall Cycle: August 28
  • Winter Cycle: November 28
  • Spring Cycle: February 28
  • Summer Cycle: May 28

Organizations selected from the LOI process will be invited to submit a full proposal. We encourage applicants to align their requests with the Foundation’s core focus areas and clearly demonstrate community impact.

For questions on eligibility or the application process, contact us.

What We Look For in Applications

We prioritize funding for programs and projects that:


  • Demonstrate a measurable impact on Rhode Island communities.
  • Foster collaboration and partnerships with local organizations.
  • Focus on underserved or marginalized populations.
  • Align with our core mission and values.
  • Show transparency and accountability in program management and use of funds.

Apply for Funding

If your organization shares our vision of building stronger, more equitable communities, we encourage you to apply for funding. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, and selected recipients will receive tailored support to help maximize their impact.
